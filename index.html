Hulk
Super Heroes
The Hulk is a popular superhero from Marvel Comics, created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby. He first appeared in "The Incredible Hulk" #1 in May 1962⁵. The character is known for his immense strength and green skin, which he gains after transforming from his alter ego, Dr. Robert Bruce Banner, a brilliant physicist.
The transformation occurs when Banner is subjected to emotional stress, often leading to destructive rampages. The Hulk's strength increases proportionally with his anger, making him one of the most powerful beings in the Marvel Universe⁵.
Over the years, the Hulk has been portrayed in various media, including TV series and movies. Notable actors who have played the Hulk include Lou Ferrigno, Eric Bana, Edward Norton, and Mark Ruffalo¹. The character has also been a key member of the Avengers and has had numerous storylines exploring his complex relationship with his alter ego, Bruce Banner⁵.

Captain America, also known as Steve Rogers, is a beloved superhero from Marvel Comics. Created by Joe Simon and Jack Kirby, he first appeared in “Captain America Comics” #1 in 19411. Steve Rogers was originally a frail young man who was transformed into a super-soldier through an experimental serum during World War II1. Equipped with a virtually indestructible shield and an American flag-inspired costume, Captain America fought against the Axis powers, including his arch-nemesis, the Red Skull1. After being frozen in ice for decades, he was revived in modern times and became a key member of the Avengers1.
